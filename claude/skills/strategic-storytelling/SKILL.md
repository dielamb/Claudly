# Strategic Storytelling

## Step 1 — Identify the Hero (do this first)

Answer before writing anything:
- Who is the customer? (specific, not "everyone")
- What is their struggle in the Old World?
- What does winning look like for THEM?

Cannot answer → stop and ask for more context.

## Step 2 — Five Acts (all required)

**Act 1 — Old World**
Current broken state. Make the audience feel the pain.
Must be: relatable, specific, visceral. Not abstract.

**Act 2 — Insight**
What changed in the world that makes action necessary NOW.
Must answer: "Why now?" — no answer = no urgency, rewrite.

**Act 3 — New World**
What becomes possible because of that change.
Must be: concrete and desirable. The destination, not the journey.

**Act 4 — Stakes**
What early movers gain. What laggards lose.
Real stakes, not manufactured fear.

**Act 5 — Your Role**
How your product enables the customer's victory.
Product = weapon/guide, never protagonist.

## Step 3 — Audience Alignment (Duarte)

- Start where the audience IS
- Move them to where they COULD BE
- End with one STAR moment (Something They'll Always Remember)

## Step 4 — Delivery Checklist

- [ ] "Why now?" answered clearly
- [ ] Concrete examples used (specific > abstract)
- [ ] Emotional AND logical appeal present
- [ ] ONE clear call to action at the end

## Output

Deliver narrative structured by acts, then:
- One-sentence pitch version
- STAR moment / key quote
